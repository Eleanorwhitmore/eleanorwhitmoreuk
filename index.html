About Eleanor

My Story

I did not set out to become a book marketing specialist.

Like many people in this industry, I stumbled into it by noticing patterns that others seemed to miss. It started with a simple question I could not stop thinking about. Why do some books with excellent reviews and passionate readers stay completely invisible, while others with seemingly weaker content gain momentum and find their audience?

I became obsessed with understanding the difference. I started analyzing book after book, category after category, author after author. I looked at BSR patterns, category placements, keyword strategies, review velocities, also-bought carousels, pricing structures, blurb constructions, cover signals. Every data point I could observe.

Gradually, a pattern emerged. The difference between visible books and invisible books almost never came down to writing quality. It almost never came down to marketing budget. It came down to positioning. The visible books were positioned where their natural readers could find them. The invisible books were placed in the wrong categories, targeting the wrong keywords, signaling the wrong genre expectations.

Over the past decade, I have worked with more than 200 authors across every major genre. I have analyzed thousands of books. I have tracked the results of hundreds of positioning changes. And through all of that, I have developed a methodology that consistently produces results because it is based on observable data and testable strategy, not guesswork.

Amazon and Metadata Optimization

Categories · Keywords · Algorithm · Listing

This is the foundation of everything. Before any promotional effort can work, before any algorithmic momentum can build, the structural foundation of your Amazon listing must be correct. Most authors have never properly optimized their metadata. Category selection, keyword architecture, listing structure, series metadata, A+ Content strategy. These are the invisible levers that determine whether Amazon surfaces your book to readers or buries it.

Amazon has over 16,000 book categories. You can only be in 3 at a time. Being in the wrong categories means being invisible to the readers who would love your book. Being in the right categories means appearing exactly where your natural readers browse. A single category change can produce dramatic visibility improvements. Pair that with a keyword architecture built around what readers actually search for, and the effect compounds.

Category Repositioning

Full audit of Amazon's category tree, identification of better-fit subcategories, competition assessment, and formal KDP category change requests with follow-up.

Keyword Architecture

Deep research into what your readers actually search for, prioritized keyword lists built around real search behavior, and complete backend implementation.

Description Optimization

Book description rewritten using proven conversion structure. A hook that stops the scroll, emotional momentum through the body, and a close that drives purchase.

A+ Content Strategy

Enhanced listing content that places your book visually alongside comparison titles your readers already love, increasing trust and conversion rate.

Series Metadata Correction

Series designation, cross-linking, and architecture that signals to Amazon and to readers that this is a continuing world worth entering.

Also-Bought Analysis

Assessment of your recommendation carousel to identify drift from your natural audience and strategies to realign the algorithm's reader associations.

Launch and Relaunch Strategy

Pre-Launch · Launch Week · Post-Launch · Momentum

The first 30 days after publication are critical for Amazon's algorithm. This is when the algorithm is actively learning what kind of readers want your book and whether it should recommend it to more people. Without strategic momentum-building during this window, even excellent books fade into algorithmic obscurity. The algorithm essentially decides that your book is not worth recommending because it did not see enough sustained interest.

I build research-driven launch plans based on market analysis, not generic checklists. Pre-launch positioning, review velocity coordination, launch week promotional stacking, and post-launch momentum strategy that sustains visibility beyond the initial spike. For books that have already launched without proper strategy, I develop relaunch approaches that treat the book as a new release entering the algorithm fresh.

Pre-Launch Positioning

Categories and keywords locked before release, competitive positioning established, pre-launch audience building tactics implemented.

Review Velocity Plan

ARC coordination, reviewer targeting, and timeline strategy that builds the review volume Amazon's algorithm needs to register genuine reader interest.

Launch Week Coordination

Promotional stacking strategy for maximum launch week impact, including promo site scheduling, social timing, and email sequence coordination.

Post-Launch Momentum

Sustained visibility strategy for weeks 2 through 8, when most launches fail. Measurable checkpoints and adjustment triggers built into the plan.

Series and Backlist Revival

Read-Through · Funnels · Backlist · Revenue

For series authors, read-through rate is the most important metric that most people ignore. A healthy read-through rate from Book 1 to Book 2 should be 50 to 70 percent or higher. If yours is below 30 percent, you are leaving enormous amounts of money on the table. A 10 percent improvement in read-through can effectively double your per-reader revenue.

For backlist titles, the book market changes constantly. New categories emerge. Reader communities grow and evolve. Keywords that nobody searched for three years ago might be trending today. A book that was poorly positioned at launch can find new life with updated metadata, refreshed categories, and reintroduction to communities that have grown since the original release.

Read-Through Analysis

Calculation of your actual read-through rates, identification of where readers are dropping off, and diagnosis of the structural causes behind each drop point.

Funnel Optimization

Back-matter audit and rebuilding with embedded purchase links, newsletter CTAs, and series continuation copy written in the voice of your world.

Backlist Repositioning

Category and keyword refresh for older titles, metadata updates, and a relaunch strategy that treats the book as a new release entering a market that has grown since publication.

Pricing Strategy

Permafree strategy, price pulsing, and series pricing optimization designed to maximize both read-through and total series revenue.
